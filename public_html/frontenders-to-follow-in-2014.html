Follow these guys on Twitter for everything frontend in 2014

Not that these people really need any more followers, but here's a list of people who will provide you with all the useful links, wisdom and humor you'll need as a frontend developer in 2014. And of couse, a few gifs of cats and whatnot will surely be provided.

Note: this list will be extended over the next few weeks!

  • 1.

    Rachel Nabors @rachelnabors

    Interaction & experience designer, CSS junkie, cartoonist.

    Rachel is an AMAZING cartoonist, and mixes this with her passion for writing awesome CSS and JavaScript experiments.

    I saw Rachel present a music video she made with CSS animation last year which was very impressive, but unfortunately we don't all share Rachel's skills in producing tons of perfect frame-by-frame image sprites!

  • 2.

    Phil Hawksworth @philhawksworth

    Technical Director at R/GA London.

    Phil is an avid promoter of anything which can boost web page performance and accessibility, and has recently been touring the speaking circuit with his I can smell your CMS talk, in which he covers exactly what not to do when building a fast responsive website.

    We're lucky Phil keeps his social networks separate, because he Instagrams nothing but pictures of his cats.

  • 3.

    Brad Green @bradlygreen

    Engineering director at Google, managing development of AngularJS.

    Brad tweets awesome stuff about AngularJS, and is a great supporter of anyone that makes useful tools or writes interesting articles about the framework.

    How he still only has 4k followers I don't know, that's sure to become 10k+ in 2014 as the popularity of AngularJS skyrockets!

  • 4.

    Remy Sharp @rem

    Web developer and creator of JSBin.

    You're probably already following him. Remy is more than just a frontend developer. Really knowledgeable about all things web, makes his way tidily around the speaking circuit, make sure you follow him if you're not already!

  • 5.

    Jake Archibald @jaffathecake

    Developer advocate for Google Chrome.

    An expert in everything web performance related, Jake works at Google, specifically on Chrome, and knows everything there is to know about how web browsers slice and dice our code.

    I think we can all agree if the whole world used Chrome it would be a much nicer place.

  • 99.

    Andy Shora @andyshora

    Way down the list, there's me! I'm a Frontend Developer based in London.

    I tweet about AngularJS, JavaScript, frontend performance tips, and anything technical I find interesting from Hacker News. Obviously I also tweet stuff I find on Reddit which is anything more than mildly funny.
