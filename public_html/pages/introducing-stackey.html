Introducing Stackey.com

Stackey.com is a place where you can stack just about anything. Discover new stacks of products that go together like peas and carrots, as recommended by people who care.

It's a bit like Pinterest, but it's exclusively for products. Not a whole board of products, mind... just a stack of upto 5 special things!

What do you mean 'stack' things?

In technology, we often refer to stacks as the layers of different technologies we use to implement various parts of an application. I want people to be able to stack anything. Hell, you're probably stacking things and you aren't even aware of it!

  • Do you have a few products you use on your face each morning?
  • Your favourite gym kit, it all goes together, right?
  • How about those nice pieces of kit you bought to make a proper coffee at home?

These are all stacks! They can be recommended to friends, and are probably really nice products if YOU bought them :-)

Need some examples?

Here's a couple of stacks which will help explain the concept at a glance:

  • Checkout my Coffee Making Stack, which shows all the things I've fine-tuned over the years to make awesome coffee in my kitchen at home.

  • Or Elsa's Pasta Making Stack, featuring some great tools to make proper Italian pasta.

It's for discovery.

Like Pinterest, but for products.

For a while I found myself recommending Shaving products to friends, so I created My Shaving Stack. This contains some excellent products I'd recommend to any guy who wants an upgrade from the cheap supermarket shave. Check it out...

It features 5 items, which are named in the following way to make it easy to see how the things fit together to form a stack:

  1. The Ease Oil (ClarinsMen Shave Ease Oil)
  2. The Shaving Cream (Proraso Shaving Cream)
  3. The Brush (Edwin Jagger Best Badger Brush)
  4. The Razor (Merkur Heavy Duty Slant Bar Safety Razor)
  5. The Blades (Feather Double Edged Razor Blades)

It's early days, but it looks bright.

I'm thrilled so many of you have already joined, and YOU, reading this, I would love for you to sign up and have a go at creating some stacks too!

I'll be following up this article with some tech and UX details, where I hope to explain how everything was made, including some design decisions around the UI, and a few little performance hacks. For the geeks amongst you, it's not to be missed!
